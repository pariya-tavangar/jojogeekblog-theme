Philosophy

Why break what already works? Because growth happens at the edges.

I'm driven by the belief that digital experiences have become too homogeneous. We've settled into patterns that prioritize efficiency over wonder, predictability over discovery.

My approach is to question every assumption: Why should navigation always be at the top? Why should content flow linearly? What if a website could feel more like an exploration than a transaction?

The inclusion of both light and dark themes in this unconventional design demonstrates how even the most experimental interfaces can adapt to user preferences while maintaining their unique character.

When I'm not breaking websites, I study architectural theory, write experimental poetry, and collect vintage computing manuals that imagined very different digital futures than the one we inhabit.
